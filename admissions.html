Playgroup

Age 2-3 years

Playgroup is a child’s first step toward independent schooling, helping them smoothly transition from the comfort of their primary caregiver. Our play-based curriculum encourages children to explore, discover, and engage in a variety of sensory, creative, and physical activities, while gradually developing their ability to focus and complete tasks.


Our learning objectives focus on developing early literacy skills while encouraging independence in young learners. Special events and outdoor field trips create meaningful, memorable experiences, and a range of co-curricular activities supports holistic development. We maintain a 10:1 student–teacher ratio, with each session lasting three hours.

Nursery

Age 3-4 years

Our Nursery curriculum builds a strong foundation for future learning within a safe, nurturing environment where every child feels valued, confident, and independent. The classrooms emphasize early literacy while seamlessly integrating co-curricular activities into everyday learning.


Designed to support holistic development, our curriculum uses play-based learning to encourage children to ask questions, plan investigations, explore outcomes, and draw logical conclusions, with teachers guiding and supporting them throughout the process. Reading becomes a key focus in the Nursery programme, while experiential learning through events and field trips adds excitement and depth to their education. We maintain a 10:1 student–teacher ratio, and each session runs for four hours.

Pre-Primary 1 & 2

Age 4-6 years

In PP1 and PP2, we adopt an inquiry-based approach that nurtures children’s curiosity and encourages experiential learning. The environment serves as a third teacher, thoughtfully designed with purposeful materials that support relationships, communication, collaboration, and exploration through play. Carefully selected resources are added to inspire creativity, problem-solving, experimentation, and open-ended learning.


The curriculum is organised into interdisciplinary units that integrate multiple subject areas, making learning engaging and meaningful. Through hands-on activities, play, and project work, children actively investigate their interests, discover new ideas, and develop their own theories. Teachers support each child’s unique learning style, interests, and talents, fostering learner agency and emphasising the development of communication and thinking skills.


Our curriculum covers key areas including Language & Literacy, Numeracy, Science, Social Studies, Creative and Visual Arts, and Personal, Social, Emotional, and Physical Development. Reading is a core component of these programmes, and we follow a structured reading plan to support children’s learning at school.
